These articles are our retrospective on our [AI alignment course](https://aisafetyfundamentals.com/alignment/) iteration, beginning in March 2024.[1] We identified different perspectives we'd like to analyse the course from, and broke up our analysis into three parts:

- [Systems and processes](https://bluedot.org/blog/ai-alignment-march-2024-retro-systems-and-processes/)
- [Curriculum development](https://bluedot.org/blog/ai-alignment-march-2024-retro-curriculum-development/)
- [Getting good people on the course](https://bluedot.org/blog/ai-alignment-march-2024-retro-good-people/)

## Who is this for?

It's primarily written for ourselves, and published in line with our philosophy of [working in public](https://bluedot.org/blog/working-in-public/). However, we expect it could be useful to:

- People considering [applying to our courses](https://apply.aisafetyfundamentals.com/alignment), to understand the kind of work we put in to make them an excellent experience (and what the experience is like for participants).
- People considering [joining our team](https://bluedot.org/careers/), to better understand the kind of work we do.
- People [running local versions of our courses](https://bluedot.org/running-versions-of-our-courses/), or otherwise getting into course design or running, to copy what we did well and avoid the mistakes we made.
- People [considering giving us funding](https://bluedot.org/blog/case-for-support-2024-2026/), to help explain how we might use this funding to deliver on supporting people developing knowledge, skills and connections to pursue high-impact careers.
